considered.
In this paper, a new distributed secondary control scheme
ispresentedforDCmicrogrid.Itisabletonotonlyguarantee
proper current sharing, but also maintain the DC bus voltage
at the nominal value. The contributions of this paper mainly
lie in the following aspects:
• A new parameter ‘virtual voltage drop’ is defined from
the droop gain and line resistance. It is shown that the
average value of this new parameter plays a key role in
proper current sharing and voltage restoration;
• For each converter, by only exchanging information with
its neighbours, a new algorithm is designed to enable
eachconvertertoestimatetheaveragevalueofthenewly
defined parameter. This forms our proposed distributed
secondary control scheme;
• Itisprovedtheoreticallythattheproposeddistributedsec-
ondary control scheme can ensure both current sharing
and voltage restoration. Compared with existing results,
theproposedschemehastwoadvantageousfeatures:1)It
is simpler and easier to implement as it does not require
the DC bus voltage as the feedback signal. 2) It is able
to handle both resistance loads and CPLs.
Both simulations and experiments are conducted to illustrate
the effectiveness of the proposed control scheme.
